# How to Play Farkle

Reference summary adapted from [Cardgames.io — How to play Farkle](https://cardgames.io/blog/how-to-play-farkle/). Farkle is played in many regional variants; this describes the simpler ruleset used on that site.

## Objective

Be the first player to **bank more than 10,000 points**. Turns alternate while players try to score melds and decide when to stop rolling (bank) versus risking a “farkle” and losing unbanked points for the round.

## Equipment

Six standard dice.

## Turn flow: roll, score, bank

1. **Roll** all six dice at the start of your turn.
2. After each roll, **set aside** dice that form scoring melds (see below). You must set aside **at least one** scoring die each roll; beyond that you choose which scoring dice to keep and which to re-roll.
3. **Re-roll** any dice not set aside to try for more melds—or **bank** to add your round total to your permanent score and end your turn. Banked points cannot be lost.
4. Pass the dice to your opponent; their turn begins.

### Melds are per throw only

Melds **do not carry over** between rolls. Dice scored on separate throws are separate melds.

Example: You roll two 1s and set them aside (100 + 100 = **200**). On the next roll you roll another 1. That is **not** three 1s for 1,000—all three faces must appear on the **same** throw. You keep a separate single 1 worth **100**, for **300** total from those dice, not a triple-ones meld.

## Farkle

If, after a roll, **no die** can be part of a scoring meld, you **farkle**. Your turn ends immediately, you **lose all unbanked points** from that round, and play passes to your opponent. Each extra re-roll increases farkle risk.

## Hot dice

If **every die** you just rolled can be used in melds (all six dice score), you have **hot dice**. You may pick up **all six** dice and roll again, continuing the same turn. Points already earned this round stay in the “at risk” pool until you bank or farkle.

## End of game

The game ends when a player **ends a turn** with **more than 10,000 points banked**. You are not forced to bank as soon as you could win; you may keep rolling if the table allows it—but banking to win is the usual goal.

## Scoring melds

| Meld | Points |
|------|--------|
| Each **1** | 100 |
| Each **5** | 50 |
| Three **1**s | 1,000 |
| Three **2**s | 200 |
| Three **3**s | 300 |
| Three **4**s | 400 |
| Three **5**s | 500 |
| Three **6**s | 600 |
| **Four** of a kind | 1,000 |
| **Five** of a kind | 2,000 |
| **Six** of a kind | 3,000 |
| **Three pairs** (includes four-of-a-kind + a pair) | 1,500 |
| **Straight** 1–2–3–4–5–6 | 2,500 |
