# Farkle — Farkle.games “official rules” summary

Reference summary from [Official Farkle Rules](https://farkle.games/official-rules/) — **farkle.games** (site that publishes Farkle mobile apps). Scoring for **three pairs** and the **straight** is **lower** than many folk / retail tables; the page also documents a **triple-Farkle** penalty.

## Objective

Have the **highest score above 10,000** on the **final round** of play. When a player reaches **10,000+**, that player “goes out”; **each** other player gets **one more turn** to beat the high score (the page notes this **varies by families and friends**).

## Turn flow

- Roll six dice, set aside scoring combinations.
- After each roll, **at least one** scoring die must be selected.
- Either **bank** the turn’s points into your total and pass, or **re-roll** remaining dice.
- **Farkle**: no scoring dice on a roll → **lose all points earned that turn** (round).
- **Hot dice**: all six dice used for scoring → roll **all six again**; may repeat. (Under **Gameplay Variations**, the site notes some groups require a mandatory re-roll when all six score.)

## Per-roll scoring (main chart)

Dice from **different rolls** cannot be combined into one meld (the site illustrates 5-5 then 5 as three singles, not three 5s).

| Combination | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Each **1** | 100 |
| Each **5** | 50 |
| Three of a kind | **Face value × 100** (e.g. 2-2-2 = 200) |
| Three **1**s | **1,000** (exception to the ×100 pattern) |
| Three pairs | **750** |
| Straight **1–2–3–4–5–6** | **1,000** |

The main page lists scoring for singles, three of a kind, three pairs, and the straight; it does **not** put four/five/six-of-a-kind in the primary chart.

## Triple Farkle penalty

If you **Farkle three times in a row**, you **lose 1,000 points** from your banked total (“Ouch!”). The **Gameplay Variations** section notes some groups use **500** or **no** penalty.

## Optional variations (listed on same page)

The site documents common house rules, including: **break-in** scores (e.g. 300 to “get on the board”); three pairs scored as **0 / 500 / 750 / 1,500**; **three aces** as 1,000 vs 100; **4/5/6 of a kind** as multiples of the three-of-a-kind score; straight **1,000–1,500**; play to **5,000** or **20,000**; **two triplets** as **2,500**; **piggybacking** using the previous player’s leftover dice.
