# Zilch — Playr.co.uk rules summary

**Zilch** is the same broad family as Farkle (push-your-luck with six dice, “lose the turn” on a dead roll). Rules differ in details. This file summarizes [Rules of Zilch](https://zilch.playr.co.uk/rules.php) on **playr.co.uk** (2007–2008).

Use this when you want **doubling four-of-a-kind** math, a **300** banking threshold, or the **third-zilch** penalty — not identical to typical Farkle boxed rules.

## Turn flow

1. Roll all six dice to start the turn.
2. If any dice score, you **must take some** of those points before rolling again.
3. If **no** dice score → you **zilch**: **wipe** points taken so far this turn; turn ends. **Third consecutive zilch** → **lose 500** from your banked score.
4. Once you have taken **at least 300** points this turn, you may **bank** and end the turn, or keep rolling.
5. Re-roll any dice you **did not** use in your chosen scoring combination(s).
6. If you score with **all six** dice → **free roll** of all six (same idea as “hot dice”).

## Scoring guide (from Playr)

- **1s**: 100 each; **5s**: 50 each.
- **Three of a kind**: 1s = **1,000**; 2s–6s = **200–600** (face × 100 pattern, same as common Farkle).
- **Four or more of a kind**: start from the three-of-a-kind score, then **double for each extra die** beyond three (Playr’s examples: four 2s → 400; five 2s → 800; six 3s shown as 300 → 600 → 1,200 → 2,400 in their illustration chain).
- **Special rolls** (each also grants a **free roll** per Playr):
  - Straight **1–6** (any order): **1,500**
  - **Three pairs**: **1,500**
  - **No scoring dice** on a roll: **500** and a **free roll** (their “special” example — unusual vs. a normal zilch; read their scoring guide on the site for exact treatment).

**One combination per die:** Dice can only count toward **one** scoring combination on that roll.
